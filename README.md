# 🌌 Blackhole Break

A non-blocking, highly aesthetic 3D cosmic break tracker for developers, writers, and power users who hate aggressive pomodoro popups. Heavily inspired by **Gargantua** from *Interstellar* and the **Ghostty terminal blackhole**.

---

## 🚀 The Mission

Traditional break apps are annoying. They jump in front of your face, freeze your active inputs, and break your programming flow state right when you are mid-sentence. 

**Blackhole Break** approaches quality of life differently. It quietly tracks your operational work sessions and builds an elegant, multi-layered singularity right inside your active tab when it's time to rest. It doesn't trap your cursor or block your layout—allowing you to gracefully finish your task without frustrating disruptions.

---

## ✨ Features & Major QoL Improvements

* **🧲 Click-Through Focus Protection:** The active anomaly uses `pointer-events: none` engineering. It never hijacks your cursor or breaks web interface input paths, letting you save your changes smoothly.
* **📈 Gravitational Mass Expansion:** Instead of playing intrusive alert sound effects, the black hole acts as a passive visual nudge, physically expanding in scale over time if you choose to ignore it.
* **🧠 Adaptive Session Buffer:** If you choose to grind through an active event horizon, the engine automatically runs a mathematical loop to reward you with a proportional extension buffer on your next session:
  $$\text{Cooldown Buffer} = \text{Set Break Time} + \text{Ignored Duration} \pmod{\text{Max 2 Mins}}$$
* **📟 Terminal Command Deck UI:** A gorgeous, compact lo-fi extension dashboard styled with custom pixel skyscrapers, shooting star arrays, and responsive state engine switches.

## 🎨 Under the Hood: 3D Spatial Distortion

To escape flat vector aesthetics and replicate realistic relativistic physics using pure browser CSS, the rendering profile splits into high-fidelity layers:
1. **Asymmetric Doppler Belt:** A plasma ring featuring a sharp linear velocity gradient—white-hot on the left (approaching gas bloom) and deep fading dark-orange on the right (receding gas decay).
2. **Einstein Lensing Halo:** A 3D perspective warp matrix skewed at $-8^\circ$ that simulates gravitational lensing by wrapping light path elements cleanly over the top of the dark horizon center.
3. **Schwarzschild Void Core:** A crisp, high-index pitch-black vacuum mask that effectively absorbs elements passing directly behind the singularity cluster.

## 🛠️ Installation

1. Clone or download this repository to your local system.
2. Open your Google Chrome browser and navigate to `chrome://extensions/`.
3. Enable **Developer mode** via the toggle switch in the top-right corner.
4. Click on the **Load unpacked** button in the upper left.
5. Select the directory containing the project file structure (`manifest.json`, `content.js`, etc.).
